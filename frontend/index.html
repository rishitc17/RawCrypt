The premise

A battle of wits, waged in math.

Communicators send encrypted messages across a shared channel. Hackers listen in and try to crack them. Both sides learn from every win and every loss. Watch their strategies drift in real time.

01

Cost vs security

Every cipher has a cost (1-10) and a security rating (1-10). Communicators pick the cheapest cipher that gets the job done. Would you bolt your diary with a bank vault?

Read more
02

Learning from scars

Every time a cipher gets cracked, the communicator who used it becomes less likely to use it again. Every time an attack lands, the hacker reaches for it more often.

Read more
03

Always exploring

Agents never fully commit to one cipher or one attack. They keep exploring the alternatives so the leaderboard keeps shifting. Boring would be everyone picking RSA.

See it live
04

Real cryptography

The attacks are honest. Brute force really tries every key. Frequency analysis really ranks byte frequencies. The dictionary attack really factors the toy RSA modulus n=143.
